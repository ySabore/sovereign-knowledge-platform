# venture discovery guide — law firms

## goal of the first call
confirm whether the firm has a painful knowledge-retrieval problem, enough reusable internal content, a buyer/owner, and a safe pilot path.

## 15-minute call structure
1. current workflow (3 min)
2. pain and cost of delay (4 min)
3. systems, content, and security constraints (4 min)
4. pilot fit and next step (4 min)

## opening
before i show anything, i would love to understand how your team currently finds prior briefs, templates, clauses, checklists, and internal know-how when speed matters.

## core discovery questions
### current workflow
- when an attorney or paralegal needs a prior example, where do they look first?
- what systems hold most of the useful content today?
- what usually makes finding the right prior work slow or frustrating?

### pain and business value
- where do you see the most repeat searching or duplicated work?
- who feels this pain most: partners, associates, paralegals, operations?
- if this improved, what would change first: turnaround time, consistency, onboarding, write-offs, or something else?

### content readiness
- what document types would matter most in a pilot: briefs, motions, templates, research memos, checklists, policies?
- roughly how much internal content is already available and organized enough to start with?
- are there one or two practice groups where reuse is especially high?

### security and deployment
- what are your non-negotiables around confidentiality and client data?
- would this need to live fully inside your environment, or is a controlled hosted option acceptable?
- are there any systems or repositories that are off-limits for a first pilot?

### buying process
- who besides you would need to weigh in on a pilot?
- is this more of an innovation budget, operations budget, or practice-group decision?
- what would make a first pilot feel low-risk and worth trying?

## qualification signals
### strong fit
- they describe repeated searching across shared drives or dms
- they have strong internal documents but weak reuse
- they worry about confidentiality with public ai tools
- one practice group can pilot quickly
- someone owns knowledge, operations, or innovation

### weak fit
- little reusable internal content
- no clear workflow pain
- they mainly want generic legal research from public sources
- no internal owner and no path to a pilot

## good pilot wedge
start with one practice area and 3–5 high-value document sets:
- top templates
- frequently reused briefs or motions
- checklists / playbooks
- internal memos or reference docs

## recommended next step if qualified
propose a focused follow-up around:
- pilot scope
- source systems / sample documents
- confidentiality and deployment requirements
- success metric for the first 30 days

## simple close
this sounds like a real fit area. rather than keep this abstract, the best next step is a working session around one practice area, the document sources you would start with, and what a low-risk pilot would need to prove. 

## what venture should capture in notes
- buyer and internal champion
- most painful search/reuse workflow
- target practice area
- document types available
- deployment/security constraints
- urgency trigger
- next meeting owner and date
